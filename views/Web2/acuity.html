Visual Acuity - clarity or sharpness of vision

  • When you reach middle age (40+), your visual acuity will probably not change, but you may lose your near vision.
  • Your eyes' lenses become less flexible and it becomes harder to change focus from objects that are far away to close objects.
  • Diopter equivalency compared to normal (0: 20/20):
    • MILD can see clearly out to 50 cm (20")
      • -1: 20/40
    • MEDIUM can see clearly out to 25 cm (10")
      • -2: 20/80*
      • -3: 20/150*
      • -4: 20/300*
    • HIGH (and higher risk for detached retina)
      • -5: 20/400*
      • -6: 20/500*

Statistics

  • Only about 35% of adults have 20/20 vision naturally (without the aid of glasses, contacts, or corrective surgery).
  • With corrective measures, approximately 75% of adults have 20/20 vision.
  • People are considered “legally blind” if their corrected vision (with eyeglasses or contacts) is 20/200 or worse.
  • In almost every state, visual acuity of 20/40 or better is required for an unrestricted driver's license.
  • Prevalence of myopia in adults (20-59) measured in diopter spherical equivalent:
    • 50% with ≤ -0.5
    • 37% with ≤ -1
    • 8% with ≤ -5 (severe, and higher risk for detached retina)
Data from NCBI
