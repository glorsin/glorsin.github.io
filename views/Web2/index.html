Visual Acuity

  • An eye chart measures visual acuity, which is the clarity or sharpness of vision.
  • “20/20 vision” refers to normal vision, not perfect vision.
  • A person with 20/20 vision can see what an average individual can see on an eye chart when they are standing 20 feet away.
  • For example, if you have 20/30 vision, it means your vision is worse than average. When you are standing 20 feet from the chart, you can read letters that most people see when they are 30 feet away.

Detached Retina

  • The retina is a layer of nerve cells lining the back wall inside the eye.
  • This layer senses and sends signals to the brain so you can see.
  • A detached retina is when the retina lifts away from the back of the eye making vision blurry.
  • It is a medical emergency when this happens with the possibility of losing sight in that eye permanently.
